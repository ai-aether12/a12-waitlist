This is harder than it looks.

Jamie Yu-Ramos
Founder

I have ADHD. I was diagnosed many years ago, and through a lot of therapy and years of self-reflection, I learned to accept the fact that I was born with it. It is the only explanation for how much of my life has unfolded.

It is occasionally demoralizing, even to this day, to know that ADHD was part of me for so long. If I had been diagnosed when I was young, I am certain my life would have been quite different in much-needed positive ways. But had that been the case, I wouldn't be a husband and father now, and I wouldn't be here sharing this with you.

Why then?

Because I'm building something to help me live my life better — and I think it will help you as well. And that is what matters most to me now.

I would very much like to stop beating myself up for forgetting another thing I meant to start — and then finishing half of it.

I started Aether because I want the relief of knowing that things are already in motion. That my life isn't quietly piling up while I'm spinning my wheels, and that the people counting on me aren't left waiting.

Aether won't wait for you to deal with things. It will move before you have to.

ADHD, for all of its positives, is exhausting. And I'm tired of being tired.

Awareness was never the problem.
