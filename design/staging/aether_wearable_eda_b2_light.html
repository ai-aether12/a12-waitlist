Architectural notes
B-2 EDA scenario — design decisions and constraints
Signal source
EDA (electrodermal activity / skin conductance). Elevated readings during sustained document dwell without edit activity indicate approach-avoidance conflict — the physiological signature of being in front of the task and unable to initiate.
Compatible wearables
Samsung Galaxy Ring, Pixel Watch, Fitbit Sense 2. Wearable class selection is a POC-4+ decision gated on WS-4. Not in current POC scope.
Detection trigger
All four required: (1) document open and in foreground, (2) dwell time above threshold (30–40 min, calibrated per user), (3) edit activity below baseline, (4) EDA elevation above personal baseline for ≥ 5 min. Prevents false positives from normal reading or distracted browsing.
Intervention constraint
One sentence only. The opening line is drafted by the system; the user owns what follows. The boundary is enforced visually (dashed line) and in copy ("that's all. the rest is yours."). The goal is to reduce activation energy for initiation — not to draft the document.
Distinction from HRV hold
HRV hold = capacity is low, defer high-friction tasks. EDA avoidance = capacity is normal, initiation is blocked for this specific task. The signal row "Recovery score · Normal · not capacity" makes this distinction explicit to the user. Different signal, different intervention, different UI state.
