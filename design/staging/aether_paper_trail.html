Log · paper trail
"Why Aether did this"
The toggle is present on every log entry by default. It is recessive — same weight as a secondary label, not a button. Users who don't want the reasoning never see it. Users who do can expand at any time after the fact.
Always available · never foregrounded
Three sections in the paper trail
Signals detected — what Aether saw. What was ruled out — alternatives considered and why they were skipped. Decision — what happened and when. The "ruled out" section is unique to the paper trail and not surfaced anywhere else in the product.
Signals · ruled out · decision
Ruled out rows use strikethrough + dim
Options that were considered but not taken are visually recessive — 55% opacity, strikethrough on the label. The user sees the reasoning without being asked to process active information. The hierarchy between "what happened" and "what didn't" is pre-attentive.
Pre-attentive hierarchy
Tier 1 actions surface here too
"Calendar buffer open · Tier 1 · auto" — the buffer was set silently before the send decision. The paper trail makes this visible without requiring a separate notification. The user can see the full picture of what happened in sequence.
Silent actions visible in paper trail

"Why now" — not "Why Aether did this"
On staged entries the action hasn't happened yet — the user is being asked to decide. The toggle label shifts from retrospective ("Why Aether did this") to prospective ("Why now"). The reasoning supports the decision, not just explains it after the fact.
Copy adapts to entry state
Outcome row changes for staged entries
"Draft staged · nothing sent · your approval required" — the outcome row isn't a completed action, it's a status. The user reads the full reasoning chain and arrives at a clear statement of what's waiting for them. The action chip below is the next step.
Reasoning → status → action
Paper trail before the decision, not after
In the Log, the paper trail is retrospective — it explains a completed action. Here it is prospective — it builds confidence before the user acts. Both are the same component. The surface determines the meaning, not the structure.
Same component · context determines meaning

Action detail · paper trail
"Full reasoning" — not "Why Aether did this"
On the action detail screen the condensed signals are already visible. The toggle label shifts to "Full reasoning" — it's an expansion of what's already there, not a reveal of something hidden. The condensed view is replaced by the full paper trail on expand, not stacked below it.
Replace · not append
Paper trail lives inside the reason card
The toggle sits within the reason card boundary. Expanding it extends the card — it doesn't break out of it. The action buttons stay below, always visible and reachable. The paper trail never pushes actions off screen.
Card-contained · actions always reachable
Same structure across all three surfaces
Signals → Ruled out → Decision. The three-section structure is identical whether you're reading past actions in the Log, evaluating a staged item in Current, or deciding from the deep link detail. Users who engage with the paper trail in one place know how to read it everywhere.
