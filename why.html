This is harder than it looks.

Jamie Yu-Ramos
Founder

I have ADHD. I was diagnosed many years ago, and through years of therapy, self-reflection, and a grudging acceptance of meds, I learned to accept the fact that I was born with it. It is the only explanation for how my life has unfolded.

It is frustrating, even to this day, to know that ADHD was always there. If I had been diagnosed when I was a kid, my life would have been quite different. But had that been the case, I wouldn't be who I am now, and I wouldn't be here sharing this with you — though putting it on the internet forever is more than a bit embarrassing and absolutely nerve-wracking.

Why then?

I'm tired of just being aware. So I'm building something to help me live my life better — and I think it will help you as well. And that is what matters most to me now.

Awareness is not the problem.

I want the relief of knowing that things are already in motion. That my life isn't quietly piling up while I'm stuck or spinning my wheels, and that the people counting on me aren't left waiting.

And I would very much like to stop beating myself up for forgetting another thing I meant to start — and then finishing only half of it once I do.

ADHD is exhausting. And I'm tired of being tired.

That's why I'm building Aether.
